# Stars Without Number: Class, Skill, and Focus Descriptions

Companion to `character-creation.md`. That file covers procedure and numbers; this one covers what the choices *mean* so a player can pick informedly.

---

## Skills

Skills are rated level-0 (competent) through level-4 (sector-best). Roll 2d6 + skill level + best applicable attribute modifier vs. a difficulty.

### Non-Psychic Skills

- **Administer.** Bureaucracy, paperwork, organizational management, legal knowledge, dealing with government agencies. The skill of making institutions work.
- **Connect.** Knowing useful people and getting them to cooperate. Finding contacts, sources, illicit goods and services, working across foreign cultures and languages. Substitutes for Talk when persuading someone you found via Connect.
- **Exert.** Trained physical exertion: running, jumping, lifting, swimming, climbing, throwing. Counts as a combat skill only for thrown attacks.
- **Fix.** Building and repairing devices. The complexity reachable depends on the character's background; a lostworlder needs study time before they can patch a fusion reactor. Also identifying what unknown tech is supposed to do.
- **Heal.** Medicine and psychology. Curing diseases, stabilizing the dying, treating mental disorders, diagnosing illness.
- **Know.** Academic and scientific facts. Planetary ecology, history, science mysteries, esoteric topics.
- **Lead.** Convincing others to follow you, even when it's a bad idea. Combat command, morale, discipline. (Talk persuades; Lead commands.)
- **Notice.** Spotting things: anomalies, ambushes, hidden objects, the emotional state of others.
- **Perform.** Stage skills: singing, dancing, acting, oratory, and so on.
- **Pilot.** Flying spaceships, driving vehicles, riding beasts, basic vehicle repair. Doesn't extend to vehicle types outside the character's background without practice.
- **Program.** Computers and comms. Programming, hacking, controlling computer-operated hardware, decryption.
- **Punch.** Combat skill for unarmed fighting. Habitual brawlers want the Unarmed Combatant focus.
- **Shoot.** Combat skill for ranged weapons: thrown rocks, bows, pistols, rifles, ship guns.
- **Sneak.** Stealth, disguise, infiltration, sleight of hand, pickpocketing, defeating security systems.
- **Stab.** Combat skill for melee weapons of any tech level.
- **Survive.** Wilderness and urban survival: food, water, shelter, dangerous fauna, difficult terrain, scrounging.
- **Talk.** Persuasion, charm, deception in conversation. (What the listener does with the conviction is their own business.)
- **Trade.** Commerce: buying, selling, sourcing illicit goods, customs negotiation, running a business.
- **Work.** Catch-all for professions and trades not covered by another skill.

### Psychic Skills

Restricted to Psychics and Partial Psychics. Each is a discipline; the skill level represents mastery of its powers.

- **Biopsionics.** Physical repair, body augmentation, shapeshifting.
- **Metapsionics.** Powers that nullify, boost, or shape other psychic abilities.
- **Precognition.** Sensing future events; controlling probability.
- **Telekinesis.** Remote control of kinetic energy: moving objects, fabricating force constructs.
- **Telepathy.** Reading and influencing sapient minds.
- **Teleportation.** Physical translocation of self and allies.

## Classes

Pick one. Class is your character's primary mode of dealing with problems.

### Expert

The non-combat specialist. Doctors, slicers, pilots, grifters, technicians, scholars. Experts are the best at their chosen skill and have more skills than the other classes.

- Once per scene, reroll any failed skill check (any skill, not just their specialty) and take the better result.
- Free level in a non-combat focus tied to the character's background, on top of the universal focus pick. The two picks may stack on the same focus to start at level 2.
- Bonus skill point per character level (non-combat, non-psychic).
- Average HP, baseline attack bonus.

### Psychic

A trained wielder of metadimensional powers. Rare in the population (one in tens of thousands), and either trained or burned out. Pick this if "wields uncanny powers" is the central concept.

- Two psychic skills as bonuses (or pick the same skill twice for level-1 + a free level-1 technique).
- An Effort score, the resource that fuels psychic abilities, scaling with WIS/CON and the highest psychic skill.
- Average HP, baseline attack bonus.

### Warrior

The combat specialist. Hiveworld thugs, soldiers, mercenaries, gengineered killers. Combat in SWN is lethal; Warriors have the best odds of surviving it.

- Free level in a combat-related focus tied to the character's background, plus the universal focus pick. The two may stack on the same focus to start at level 2.
- Once per scene, either negate a successful hit against you (no damage) or turn one of your own missed attack rolls into an automatic hit.
- +2 max HP per level (so first-level HP is 1d6+2 + CON mod).
- +1 attack bonus from level 1.

### Adventurer

The hybrid. Pick this if your concept doesn't fit neatly in one of the other three. Pick two of three "partial" classes; you get a weaker version of each chosen class's perks.

- **Partial Expert.** Free non-combat focus level. Bonus non-combat non-psychic skill point per level. (No Expert reroll.)
- **Partial Psychic.** One psychic skill at level-0; can improve only that one discipline; gains an Effort score. (Locked to the chosen discipline.)
- **Partial Warrior.** Free combat focus level. +1 attack bonus at 1st and 5th level. +2 max HP per level. (No once-per-scene combat luck.)

Two-pick combinations let you build a psionic warrior, a combat medic, a fighter-grifter, etc. without forcing the character to be either a master or nothing.

## Foci

A focus is a knack, perk, or trained capability. Each focus has level 1 and level 2 benefits; only level 1 matters for character creation. Many foci grant a bonus skill at level 1, which is rolled into your starting skills.

### Combat Foci

These are typically the bonus pick for Warriors and Partial Warriors. They can also be the universal pick for any character.

- **Armsman.** Melee and thrown weapons specialist. Bonus skill: Stab. Adds Stab level to weapon damage. Cannot stack with Gunslinger for thrown weapons.
- **Assassin.** Sudden murder, concealing weapons, point-blank ambush attacks. Bonus skill: Sneak.
- **Close Combatant.** Pistol-and-blade brawler. Bonus skill: any combat skill (Stab/Shoot/Punch). Lets you fire pistols in melee without penalty and ignore Shock damage from melee.
- **Die Hard.** Hard to kill. No bonus skill. +2 max HP per character level (retroactive). Auto-stabilizes if mortally wounded by anything below a Heavy weapon.
- **Gunslinger.** Ranged weapon specialist. Bonus skill: Shoot. Adds Shoot level to ranged weapon damage. Cannot stack with Armsman for thrown weapons.
- **Ironhide.** Innate armor (uncanny reflexes, gengineered skin, telekinetic shielding). No bonus skill. AC 15 + half level (rounded up). Does not stack with worn armor; DEX/shield modifiers still apply.
- **Savage Fray.** Wades through melee. Bonus skill: Stab. Inflicts Shock damage on adjacent enemies you didn't attack this turn.
- **Shocking Assault.** Ferocious melee attacks. Bonus skill: Punch or Stab (your choice). Your Shock damage treats all targets as AC 10.
- **Sniper.** Execution Attack specialist. Bonus skill: Shoot. Roll 3d6 drop lowest on Execution Attack and target shooting checks.
- **Unarmed Combatant.** Bare-handed killer. Bonus skill: Punch. Unarmed damage scales sharply with Punch level (1d6 at level-0 up to 1d12+1 at level-4).

### Non-Combat Foci

Typically the bonus pick for Experts and Partial Experts. Available as the universal pick for anyone.

- **Alert.** Hyper-aware. Bonus skill: Notice. Cannot be surprised; cannot be Execution-Attacked. Roll initiative twice and take the better.
- **Authority.** Charismatic command. Bonus skill: Lead. Once per day, can compel an NPC who isn't openly hostile to do something within their character.
- **Connected.** Friend in every port. Bonus skill: Connect. After a week in a non-hostile location, builds a contact web you can call on once per game day for a small favor.
- **Diplomat.** Smooth negotiator. Bonus skill: Talk. Speaks all sector languages; learns new ones in a week. Reroll 1s on negotiation/diplomacy dice.
- **Hacker.** Computer security specialist. Bonus skill: Program. Roll 3d6 drop lowest on hacking checks.
- **Healer.** Battlefield medic. Bonus skill: Heal. Stabilize the dying as an On Turn action. Roll 3d6 drop lowest on Heal checks.
- **Henchkeeper.** Recruits loyal underlings. Bonus skill: Lead. Acquire a henchman within 24 hours of arriving in any community with suitable candidates. One henchman per three character levels (rounded up).
- **Specialist.** Master of one skill. Bonus skill: any non-combat non-psychic. Roll 3d6 drop lowest on that skill. May be picked multiple times for different skills.
- **Star Captain.** Ship combat expert. Bonus skill: Lead. Your ship gets +2 Command Points per turn when you captain.
- **Starfarer.** Spike-drill navigator. Bonus skill: Pilot. Auto-succeed at spike drill checks of difficulty 10 or less.
- **Tinker.** Modifies gear. Bonus skill: Fix. Doubled Maintenance score; mods cost half credits.
- **Unique Gift.** Catch-all for a one-off ability that doesn't fit existing rules: a transhuman trait, exotic augmentation, a unique gravitic talent, etc. No bonus skill. The specifics are negotiated with the GM.
- **Wanderer.** Lifelong traveler. Bonus skill: Survive. Speaks workable basics of every common sector language. Always finds free passage to anywhere traffic goes (within an hour).

### Psychic-Restricted Foci

- **Psychic Training.** *Psychics and Partial Psychics only.* Pick a psychic discipline; raise it to level-1 (with a free level-1 technique) or pick the chosen Partial Psychic discipline if you're an Adventurer. Maximum Effort increases by one. The natural way to deepen a psychic's power.
- **Wild Psychic Talent.** *Non-psychics only.* You have a faint MES condition. Pick a psychic discipline; gain its level-0 core power (or one stand-alone level-1 technique). Treated as one point of Effort. Cannot be "torched"; doesn't carry the madness risks of untrained psychic use.

### VI (Origin Foci)

Available **only as the universal focus pick**, and **only for non-Psychic characters**. Picking one of these makes your PC a Virtual Intelligence: a non-organic, machine-housed person. Pick at most one.

- **VI: Android.** A humanoid VI body, broadly indistinguishable from organic humans without inspection. Bonus skill: any non-psychic.
- **VI: Worker Bot.** A purpose-built industrial or service VI. Bonus skill: any non-psychic (typically related to your designed function). Plus +1 to one chosen attribute's modifier (capped at +2 effective). Use this when your VI was built for one specific aptitude.
- **VI: Vehicle Bot.** A VI whose body is a vehicle: groundcar, shuttle, walker, drone. Bonus skill: Pilot.
