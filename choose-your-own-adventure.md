# Choose Your Own Adventure

The sector is large and there are gaps in it. Some of those gaps are mine to fill. Some of them I would rather you fill.

If you want to contribute something to the setting, pitch it. I will work to make it canon, sometimes exactly as proposed, sometimes after we shape it together.

## What you can pitch

**Anything.**

Trivial things: a children's game. A slang phrase that means three different things on three different worlds. A fashion trend among the urban middle class. A recipe. A myth. A song. A bad joke that everybody tells and everybody pretends they don't.

Things in between: a cult. A famous duel. A rivalry between two ports. A conspiracy theory that is mostly wrong but won't go away. A historical figure your character's grandmother used to write angry letters about.

Consequential things: an alien threat that nobody knows about yet. A mysterious fleet at the edge of the sector. A cosmic anomaly. A plague. A god.

It can be silly. It can be serious. It can be both.

## The catch

The more consequential the pitch, the less control you have over it beyond the initial idea.

A children's game stays your children's game. Tell me the rules and the names of the pieces and it's yours, more or less as you described it.

A cult survives more or less in the form you pitched, but I will probably write the leadership, the schism that nobody saw coming, and the part that turns out to have been a front for something else.

An alien threat will be very much an alien threat. You will have invented one. It will then become a thing in the campaign that I run, with a logic and a behavior that may surprise you. You do not get to control how it acts or what it wants.

The trade is straightforward. Small things stay yours. Large things are seeds, and I am the one who grows them. If you want tighter ownership over what you make, pitch smaller.

## Tying it to your character

You can absolutely connect a pitch to your character's background. Your gunner could be the inventor of the children's game. Your medic could be the only person who survived the plague. Your captain could be the niece of the angry letter-writer.

Just remember: if the thing in your background is a consequential thing, you don't control it. Your medic is not in charge of the plague. Your captain is not in charge of what their aunt's grudge actually meant. Tying a big idea to your character is fine, often great, but the idea itself stays the campaign's, not the character's.

## How to pitch

Tell me. Session zero, between sessions, in a chat, on a napkin. Rough is fine. I would rather have a sentence I can build from than a polished essay I have to wedge in. If I need more from you, I will ask.
