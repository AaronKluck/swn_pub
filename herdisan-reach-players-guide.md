# A Traveler's Guide to the Herdisan Reach

*Common knowledge among spacers, merchants, and anyone who's spent time in the rimward systems of the Astyna Tau sector. Ask three people and you'll get four opinions, but what follows is more or less what everyone agrees on.*

---

The Herdisan Reach is a chain of six inhabited systems on the rimward edge of the sector, connected by standard spike-1 drill routes. It's named for Herdis, which used to run things around here. Herdis doesn't run things anymore. These days, nobody does, which is both the Reach's greatest problem and, depending on who you ask, its greatest opportunity.

The Reach is a line, not a web. Ilanga at one end, Shakunetsu at the other, and four systems strung between them like beads on a wire. If you're coming from the Consilium Cluster, you jump to Pererin, then to Ilanga, and from there it's a straight shot rimward. If you're going the other direction, Shakunetsu is a dead end unless you've got a spike-2 drive, in which case there's a tomb world out there for the truly desperate or the truly curious.

Two alien species live in the Reach. The **Sethuri** are Daphilo's natives - reptilian, tough, and not happy about the humans who colonized their world. The **Vaelith** are tall, grey-skinned, and unsettling in a way that's hard to put your finger on. They live on Herdis and they used to help run the Hegemony. Most people outside the Reach think Vaelith are dangerous manipulators. People on Herdis think they're indispensable. Both views contain some truth.

## The Systems

### Ilanga (0506)

*Trailward end of the Reach. First stop from the Consilium.*

#### Daphilo

**What everyone knows:** Daphilo is closed. No trade, no tourism, no landing without Colonial Trade Board approval, which you won't get. The planet has a corrosive atmosphere that'll eat through a standard vac suit in hours, and the Sethuri who live there breathe the stuff like air. The human colonial administration sealed the planet after the Hegemony fell and hasn't reopened it since. Officially it's for the Sethuri's protection. Unofficially, Ilanga sits on the only route between the Reach and the Consilium, and the colonials like being the ones who decide who passes through.

**What spacers say:** The refueling station at Arrhenius 1 is your only option and they'll gouge you on the price. The Sethuri used to be raiders - real ones, with their own ships - before the Hegemony put them down. Some people say they still have weapons caches in the deep caves. The colonial governor, Viceroy Mofokeng, runs the place like her personal fief.

### Kruna (0607)

*One jump rimward from Ilanga.*

#### Manes

**What everyone knows:** Manes is the Reach's rising power and it wants you to know it. The entire planet is covered in a synthetic biosphere - crystal trees, metal insects, living soil - that the original colonists built from scratch. It's eerie and beautiful and nothing there is natural. The government, the Manes Directorate, runs a surveillance state that makes a police world look like amateur hour. Every visitor carries a transponder. Every communication goes through approved channels. The Directorate says this makes Manes the safest world in the Reach. They're probably right, for certain definitions of "safe."

**What spacers say:** Don't try to encrypt anything. Don't try to remove your transponder. Don't assume any conversation is private, because it isn't. The Directorate sells techno-organic materials and surveillance tech to half the Reach, and there's always a catch buried in the contract. They've started building their own ships - strange hybrid things with living hull plating - and they don't sell those to anyone. If you need to do business in the Reach and you value your privacy, do it somewhere else.

### Gefell & Deuol (0608)

*One jump rimward from Kruna. Three inhabited worlds.*

The busiest system in the Reach, with more orbital traffic than the rest of the cluster combined.

#### Dimos

**What everyone knows:** Dimos has billions of people, a temperate climate, and a culture built around killing. The planet's only export of consequence is lazarine - a cave fungus whose extract can heal wounds, reverse aging, and do things that TL4 medicine can't touch. It is fantastically valuable. It is also fantastically dangerous to harvest. The clans who go into the caves call it "widowmoss" and accept casualty rates that would constitute a war crime anywhere else. The harvesters don't care what you think about this. They've been doing it longer than your civilization has existed.

**What spacers say:** Don't land on the surface without a factor's introduction, or you'll be treated as fair game by whatever clan finds you first. Buy your lazarine through the Syndicate at the Factor's Compound and don't try to cut a side deal with a clan chief - the last crew that tried was found in the Pit of Names with the other dead. The food is surprisingly good if you can stomach the company.

#### Oenor

**What everyone knows:** Oenor is the cultural jewel of the Reach - and the source of most of its entertainment. The fine art gets the prestige, but it's the entertainment feeds that pay the bills: dramas, music streams, interactive narratives, all produced on Oenor and consumed across the cluster. Most of this content comes from Lumivox, a media company with an almost religious devotion to its brand. The planet is small, warm, and pleasant. It is also under the Manes Directorate's thumb: a comptroller controls physical exports and takes a heavy cut, though digital feeds flow freely under a separate deal.

**What spacers say:** The art is genuinely extraordinary and nobody can quite explain why. Something about the place gets into people's heads. The artists burn bright and burn out young - more than a few of Oenor's greatest talents have gone mad, though the locals don't like to talk about it. The Keepers - a kind of monastic order - maintain something beneath the surface that they're very secretive about. Most people assume it's a power source. Don't ask too many questions about it.

Fair warning: every third person you meet on Oenor is a "cultural ambassador" with strong opinions about your aesthetic sensibility and a following of twelve. Oenor's major export may technically be fine art, but its actual major export is unsolicited commentary on everything from hull paint choices to the way you eat soup. Memes from Oenor's creative scene propagate across the Reach at speeds that would embarrass a spike drive - last season's incomprehensible inside joke about a composer's hat is this season's catchphrase on Herdis. If someone on a station quotes something at you that sounds profound but makes no sense, it's probably from Oenor. Just nod.

#### Pittis

**What everyone knows:** Pittis has the only major spaceyard in the Reach - the Pittis Orbital Yards, capable of building capital-class hulls. This makes it the single most strategically important installation in the cluster. The planet itself is a toxic nightmare: cold, with an invasive atmosphere that seeps through seals and causes a neurological condition called the Turn. Victims of the Turn become violent, mindless husks. Most of the population lives and works in orbit. Surface postings are punishment duty.

**What spacers say:** Admiral Strand runs the yards like a military dictatorship, which is more or less what it is. If you need ship work done, the yards are excellent and the prices are fair, but everything goes through the Commissariat and they can block any order they don't like. The surface mines supply the ore, and the miners are not volunteers. Don't accept a surface rotation if you can possibly avoid it. The Turned are real, they're in the lower levels, and the seals are getting worse.

### Ezeli (0708)

*One jump rimward from Gefell & Deuol.*

#### Herdis

**What everyone knows:** Herdis was the capital of the Herdisan Hegemony, which once united the entire Reach under a single government. Those days are over. The Hegemony fractured into rival factions, and today Herdis is a world of hundreds of millions divided among at least four major camps that can't agree on who should be in charge. The Vaelith Quarter in the capital is officially neutral ground, home to the largest known community of Vaelith in the sector. The Vaelith used to advise the Hegemony; now they advise whichever faction is buying.

**What spacers say:** Register with at least two factions or you'll be seen as taking sides, which is a fast way to make enemies. Harbormaster Toure is the one person everyone respects - she controls the docking permits and she plays no favorites. The Accord Hall still stands, but its wings are divided up among the factions and the place feels more like a cold war than a parliament. Watch out for Manes agents stirring the pot. If you need a Vaelith's help, you can probably get it, but you may not enjoy what they ask in return.

#### Almavar

**What everyone knows:** Almavar is an outpost on a burning world where the terraforming failed. A few thousand people survive in pressurized habitable zones that are slowly shrinking. The place is miserable, expensive, and governed by a council that nobody trusts. If you're not bringing supplies, they don't want to see you.

**What spacers say:** Someone is pulling the strings on Almavar and it's not the people you meet at the council table. The "terraforming engines" are pretech and the rare elements they produce as waste are the only reason anyone bothers importing supplies. Prices for everything are insane. Don't expect a warm welcome.

### Seren (0808)

*One jump rimward from Ezeli.*

#### Dynaero

**What everyone knows:** Dynaero is a dying colony. It was founded by the Hegemony to mine thalline - a rare earth needed for spike drive components - and the colonists held up their end of the deal. The Hegemony didn't. When it fractured, the supply ships stopped coming, and the colony has been slowly suffocating ever since. The thalline is long gone. The atmospheric recyclers are failing. The population shrinks every year.

**What spacers say:** Everyone on Dynaero is angry and Commandant van Asch has turned that anger into a religion. She controls who leaves and she doesn't let many go. They've got the original colony charter and they believe -- to a person -- that Herdis owes them: council seats, citizenship, back pay, the works. Don't tell them they'll never collect unless you want a fight. If you need fuel, the only option is Betio 8, Dr. Mirek's orbital station. He'll sell to you, but only for barter - supplies, information, or pretech. Credits are worthless to a man who never goes anywhere.

### Shakunetsu (0908)

*End of the line. One jump rimward from Seren.*

#### Perope

**What everyone knows:** Perope is a green, pleasant world at the end of the line. Several million people living at TL2 under a legal code so complicated that nobody - possibly not even the Magistrate - fully understands it. The locals pay tribute to... someone. Where the tribute goes is not something you're allowed to ask about. Offworlders are confined to the port settlement unless you get a Magistrate's writ, which costs a tribute contribution and a lot of patience.

**What spacers say:** The laws are real and the punishments are harsh. Don't go into the deep forest. Don't ask about the tribute. Don't assume you understand what's legal and what isn't, because you don't. The people are perfectly friendly as long as you stay in the port and don't cause trouble. Some captains swear there's something living in the highlands that the locals are hiding, but that's the kind of thing captains say about any world with restricted zones. Kerulen Station on the outer gas giant is your refueling stop - the scrappers there are rough but honest.

## Beyond the Reach

The following systems lie within spike-2 range of the Reach. Most people in the Reach have heard of them. Few have been there. What follows is a mixture of common knowledge, secondhand rumor, and outright speculation.

### Pererin (0405)

*Spike-2 from Ilanga. Also reachable via standard jump from Plamen.*

#### Gallia

**What everyone knows:** Gallia is a dying ocean world. The seas are rising, the land is shrinking, and nobody has figured out how to stop it. What remains is beautiful in a way that makes you angry about what's being lost.

**What spacers say:** Gallia is beautiful and dying and there's nothing anyone seems to be able to do about it. Worth seeing once, if you're the sentimental type.

#### Mitrostak

**What everyone knows:** Mitrostak is a mining planet that recently opened to offworld trade after generations of isolation. The place runs on forced labor and export quotas, and the new openness hasn't changed the underlying ugliness.

**What spacers say:** A nasty police state that just opened up. The mining boss, Director Rask, is a thug and the Kanagyris Commonality has him wrapped in "development aid." Don't mistake the open doors for a warm welcome.

#### Valld

**What everyone knows:** Valld is a tiny outpost with technology that borders on the pretech. Its Open Market is where everyone comes to trade between the Consilium and the Reach, and Valld's ruling bureaucrats sell to all comers and favor no one. If you want to do business between the two clusters, this is where it happens.

**What spacers say:** Valld is the real prize. A TL5 outpost with a functioning pretech fabricator and orbital defenses that could flatten a fleet. The officials are polite, efficient, and will absolutely destroy you if you threaten their neutrality. The Kanagyris Commonality has an embassy here, and so does pretty much everyone else with ambitions beyond their home system.

### Plamen (0304)

*Standard jump from Pererin.*

#### Cos

**What everyone knows:** A frozen world with hundreds of millions of people living underground. Run by a Preceptor Archive that controls who gets to use what technology. There are mutants on the surface - people changed by the planet's remnant biosphere - and they're not friendly.

**What spacers say:** The Archive is a cult with a library. The mutants are getting bolder. Don't go to the surface.

### Gezgin (0408)

*Spike-2 from Kruna.*

#### Protogo

**What everyone knows:** Protogo is the world people have heard of in this system - the beastmasters. They breed animals bonded to human handlers through some kind of implant technology that nobody else can replicate. Protogo's warbeasts are famous and you can't buy one.

**What spacers say:** Worth the trip if you can afford the spike-2 fare. The beastmasters are sharp traders and the local culture is genuinely interesting. Don't try to steal the implant technology; they take it personally and they have very large animals.

#### Suranir Priddhi

**What everyone knows:** Suranir Priddhi is sealed. A closed world run by psychics who claim they can predict the end of the world. No one goes in, no one comes out.

**What spacers say:** The psychics who run it are paranoid zealots and their planet supposedly suffers some kind of apocalyptic disaster every few centuries. Most people figure that's religious nonsense. Most people haven't seen what's left of the last settlement that ignored a warning.

#### Thorhil

**What everyone knows:** Thorhil is a primitive world of billions that doesn't want visitors and enforces that preference with lethal enthusiasm.

**What spacers say:** The treaty port is a cramped box where offworlders are penned and watched, and the Wardens will kill you if you try to leave it. The locals have billions of mouths to feed and no interest in sharing.

### Raivo (1108)

*Spike-2 from Shakunetsu. Dead end.*

#### Otionei

**What everyone knows:** A tomb world. Airless. Dead for centuries, resettled by a colony that's failing. If you've got a spike-2 drive and a death wish, it's one jump past Shakunetsu.

**What spacers say:** Captain Jelen Ota runs supplies out there every few months and she says it's the most depressing place in the sector. The colonists scavenge pretech from the tombs and sell it to whoever shows up, which isn't many people. There are rumors of sealed vaults that haven't been opened, and some kind of cult on one of the moons that thinks they're the rightful heirs to the dead civilization. Ota says the place won't last another generation. Most people figure she's right.
