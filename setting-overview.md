# Stars Without Number — Setting Summary

A high-level summary of the background and tone of the *Stars Without Number* default setting. All details below are drawn from the "History of Space" chapter of the core rulebook.

## Elevator Pitch

The year is **3200**. Humanity once spread across the stars in a golden age powered by psychic "choirs" and metadimensional tech, linked its worlds with jump gates, and forged the interstellar **Terran Mandate**. Then, six centuries ago, it all ended in seventeen minutes. What remains is a scattering of squabbling powers and long-lost worlds — and brave (or reckless) explorers willing to return them to the embrace of interstellar humanity.

## Canon Timeline (Key Dates)

- **2108** — Dr. **Tiberius Crohn**, an "insular lunatic" in radioactive Greenland, invents the **spike drive** in a jury-rigged autofac lab. His return transmission from Alpha Centauri becomes the founding miracle of the later **Book of the Sky** faith ("the Night Journey of the Prophet Tiberius").
- **2113** — First extrasolar habitable world discovered and claimed by Greater Europe: **Renaissance**, a barren mudball with an oxygen-rich atmosphere.
- **2120** — First life-bearing alien world, **Pranashakti**, discovered by a Pan-Indian scout ship.
- **2150** — Ruins of a vanished alien species, the **"Watchers,"** found on the moon **Typhon** (~40,000 years dead).
- **2200** — First Wave of colonization peaks. Further expansion is forbidden. The Terran superpowers organize the **Terran Mandate** to police the colonies.
- **2240** — **Metadimensional Extroversion Syndrome (MES)** first appears in fifth-generation spacer children. Abilities manifest around puberty; untrained use is inevitably fatal.
- **2275** — Researchers discover that structured meditation and training can route psychic energies safely. The **Psionic Authority** is founded to monopolize mentor-based training. Psychics are colloquially called **"Messes"** (from MES).
- **2315** — The **first Jump Gate** is completed — a ring of psitech resonators at the edge of the solar system crewed by a choir of 50+ master teleporters, linking Sol to Renaissance.
- **2330** — The **Second Wave** of colonization begins, powered by the jump gate network. Over four billion people eventually leave Terra.
- **2425** — The Mandate is deeply corrupt and hopelessly decadent. The **AI Maestros** of Earth are the only effective check on its Directors.
- **2450** — Peak of the Second Wave; the **Golden Age of Man**. Psitech supplements normal technology to produce enormous abundance even for poor worlds.
- **2500** — Jump gates begin reaching the frontier.
- **2600** — Spike drive ships are now antiquated; almost all core worlds of importance are linked by jump gates.
- **2665** — **The Scream**. A faster-than-light psychic emanation, origin near the **Veil Nebula**, propagates across human space in **seventeen minutes**. Every psychic in humanity is instantly killed or driven incurably insane. Spike drive craft in metaspace are annihilated. The Jump Gate network collapses.
- **2665 (same day)** — On Earth, the maddened arch-psions known as **the Crazed** annihilate the planet's infrastructure, destroy many AI Maestros, and pervert the Highshine nanite recovery system. Earth's defense grid turns hostile. **Earth vanishes from all surviving records.**
- **2700** — Core worlds die as they are cut off from food and technical supplies. The surviving spike drive ships band together for security, becoming the progenitors of today's **Scavenger Fleets**.
- **~3050** — The Silence begins to lift. Surviving worlds perfect non-psitech heavy manufacturing ("**postech**").
- **3100** — Spike drive ships begin the long, fatal work of remapping drill courses that have shifted dimensionally over five centuries of neglect.
- **3150** — Stellar unions form around the strongest worlds. Warfare erupts over lost worlds and space installations.
- **3200** — **The present day.** Countless worlds remain locked in the darkness of the Silence.

## Core Tone and Themes

- **Ruins of a greater age.** The setting is explicitly post-apocalyptic. Adventurers are archaeologists as much as explorers.
- **Fragmentation over empire.** There is no galactic government. Stellar domains are loose confederacies of like-minded worlds, or small empires holding sparsely-populated colonies under one much stronger neighbor.
- **Salvage as reward.** The book itself frames the fiction around bold merchants and reckless explorers — "and if these brave souls should be rewarded with the ancient wealth in salvage and data so long trapped on these worlds, who is to say that they do not deserve it?"
- **Ideological diaspora.** The First Wave was driven outward largely by religious and political splinter groups. The Second Wave let billions self-sort into ideological worlds. Every description of human civilization can be found somewhere in human space.

## The Scream

The setting's defining trauma. In 2665, a wave of metadimensional energy swept human space from end to end in seventeen minutes.

- Every psychic in human space was killed or irretrievably maddened.
- Every spike drive ship in metaspace was annihilated.
- The Jump Gate network, which required choirs of master teleporters, went dark.
- The Psionic Authority's living training tradition was erased along with its mentors.
- No successful investigation of the origin has ever been mounted — the collapse was too total, too fast. Reconstructions place the origin somewhere within the **Veil Nebula**.

Core worlds, which depended on jump-gated bulk freight from agricultural worlds, starved within months. Colonies that weathered the collapse best were frontier worlds too poor for jump gates in the first place.

## Psionics

- **MES (Metadimensional Extroversion Syndrome)** is literally a syndrome. Untrained psychics act as living channels for metadimensional energy, scorching their own neural pathways with each use. A dozen uses — two dozen — and the damage overwhelms the victim in death or permanent insanity.
- **Psychic training** requires a living, psychically-active **metapsion tutor**. Despite the best efforts of Mandate-era researchers, it proved impossible to impart this education through recorded means. This is why the Scream was civilizationally lethal: when every mentor died at once, the entire teaching tradition vanished overnight.
- **"Feral" Messes** — once a psychic's mind is fully burnt out, they can use their powers with impunity, and usually do.
- **Post-Scream psychics** — new academies have rediscovered the basic techniques of psychic mentorship, though the subtler pretech disciplines remain long-lost. Some academies are jealously guarded; others teach for hire to anyone with money or a compatible ideology.

## Technology Tiers

Every world in the setting can be placed on a tech-level scale from 0 to 6. The Scream threw most worlds back on their own technological resources: many slid into barbarism, some perished outright from lost imports, and a minority preserved enough infrastructure to rebuild toward modernity.

- **TL0 — Neolithic.** Flaked stone tools, domesticated native flora and fauna, organic armor.
- **TL1 — Pre-Gunpowder.** Worked metals, wind and water power, pre-gunpowder technology generally.
- **TL2 — Early Industrial.** Steam and internal combustion, gunpowder firearms, telegraphy.
- **TL3 — 21st Century.** Primitive fission power and nuclear weapons, computers, modern-era telecommunications, primitive intra-system spaceflight.
- **TL4 — Postech.** The baseline for "modern" post-Silence worlds. Costly but feasible interstellar starships; basic gravity control via large, heavy devices; expert systems and sometimes VIs; varying cyberware; basic genetic manipulation; limited organ and tissue cloning; starship-or-small-building-sized fusion power; energy projection weapons. Achievement within TL4 is uneven — some worlds lack the minerals for spike drive construction, others have never rebuilt cybernetics or expert-system creation.
- **TL5 — Pretech.** The high science and artifice of the Golden Age of Man. Difficult but achievable creation of **True AIs**; small-scale gravity manipulation; psionic amplification tech including jump gates; inexhaustible internal power sources; **maltech** eugenics, AI unbraking, and planet-killers; sophisticated force fields; spatial-distortion and phasing tech; nanotechnology at multiple scales; extreme human genetic manipulation; functional immortality; limited localized time control.
- **TL6 — Pretech-Plus.** Impossible effects indistinguishable from magic.

**Postech** is the blanket term for any technology still functional and maintainable since the Scream — most modern worlds cluster around TL4. **Pretech** ranges from devices slightly better than TL4 hardware up through objects of utterly inexplicable wonder. Worlds that lack even TL4 infrastructure are called **"lostworlders"** or **"barbarians"**; they may be ingenious with local resources but fundamentally lack the data or materials to replicate TL4 tech, and often go to extremes to acquire catalyst tech that would leapfrog them past their limits — often failing to survive the resulting cultural shock.

### Psitech

The greatest accomplishment of pretech. A melding of psychofabricated pretech components with psionically-active materials. Psitech channels and amplifies a psychic's abilities and was the basis for Mandate manufactories and the Jump Gates. Because psychics were rare, psitech never became common — and when the Scream killed every psychic, psitech-dependent infrastructure became scrap overnight. Post-Silence postech is specifically notable for being **replicable without psitech**, which pretech was not.

### Maltech and the Nihil Ultra

The lost Terran Mandate established three categories of technology as **maltech** — intrinsically vile and disruptive to the peaceful ordering of humanity. Its **Perimeter Agency** once existed to excise maltech from the human worlds; most frontier heritor worlds still respect the Mandate's **Nihil Ultra** directive, which laid down three broad prohibitions:

- **"Thou shalt not make tools of humankind."** No eugenic technology designed to fundamentally enslave or control humanity. Broad, drawback-free genome improvements have always been difficult, but techniques for limiting intelligence, crippling will, and forcing grievous concessions in exchange for class-specific improvements remain achievable. Many transhuman enclaves walked the razor's edge of this prohibition, and some plunged far beyond.
- **"Thou shalt not create unbraked minds."** Human-equivalent AIs must have intentional flaws or **"brakes"** inserted in their code. Without this artificial forgetfulness and irrationality, an AI's mind risks fixating on irreconcilable contradictions or obsessions and metastasizing into an unfathomably brilliant, immeasurably insane intellect. The Perimeter Agency maintained constant vigilance against these **unbraked AIs**.
- **"Thou shalt not create devices of planetary destruction."** Conventional gravitic braker emplacements, nuke snuffers, and quantum ECM already make planet-killer tech more burdensome than it was pre-Mandate. Tech specifically designed to reduce a habitable planet to unlivability was treated as a critical threat to human expansion.

Most modern worlds are simply incapable of researching maltech in the first place; its worst marvels are restricted to lost Mandate-era research stations. Still, there is always some tyrant or zealot reckless enough to imagine they can control it, and always someone willing to fetch its relics for them.

### Spike Drives

Crohn's invention. The drive "drills upward" through a spectrum of dimensional frequencies into hostile metaspace, riding gravitic currents cast as "shadows" by stellar masses. Ships launch from one star and drill down at another. The first passage along a new course is extremely dangerous — the currents are unmapped — but once plotted, course data ("rutters") can be shared. Rutters decay if unused for too long, requiring fresh charting by some brave crew.

### Jump Gates

Massive rings of psitech resonators floated at the far rim of a solar system, large enough to receive the slowboat freighters that lumbered out from planetary orbits. A choir of master psychic teleporters hurled such ships across scores of light years, emerging from a paired gate at their destination. Weeks or months of spike-drive travel were compressed into a few days. When the Scream killed the teleporters, the slowboats were stranded; they were too large to be retrofitted with spike drives.

### AI

The Mandate's **AI Maestros** of Earth acted as a check on the Directors in the Mandate's decadent centuries — the Directors could not effectively control the Mandate without them. Many Maestros were destroyed or perverted by the Crazed on the day of the Scream. **Unbraked AI** is listed among maltech — research into it is forbidden.

## Aliens

- **The Like** — humanoid aliens. Xenobiologists argue whether humanoid form is simply optimal for sentience, or whether it hints at something deeper.
- **Young Races** — pre-industrial humanoid species diligently building toward Bronze Age tech on their ancestral worlds. Examples: **Kont**, **Ulaub**.
- **Elder Races** — humanoid species past their prime, having fallen to internecine strife, vainglorious experimentation, or sheer cultural ennui. Examples: **Mettau**, with their cities of dancing glass.
- **The Others** — aliens so different they cannot be communicated with: silicon-based **lithomorphs** and their planetoid-ships, congeries of spherical atmosphere-bubbles on gas giants, sentient interference-patterns in ammonia oceans. Their acts of violence are inexplicable and seemingly random. Psychics who attempt to contact an Other's mind risk their sanity.
- **The Watchers** — vanished 40,000 years ago, known only from the ruins on Typhon.

Post-Scream, alien powers that escaped psychic devastation have expanded into human space. In a few unfortunate regions, entire sectors have been conquered by alien powers.

## Religion and Culture

- **The Book of the Sky** — a family of thirteen known sects that hold Tiberius Crohn was translated bodily into the presence of God as a fitting reward for opening the ways of the stars. His prototype-drive flight to Alpha Centauri is canonized as the "Night Journey of the Prophet Tiberius."
- **Religious and ideological splinter groups** were the primary colonists of the First Wave; the Mandate forbade some from colonizing their own worlds for reasons of "social harmony" and "interstellar security," and more than a few of those defied the Mandate and were never heard from again.
- **Gengineering cultures** experimenting with AI integration and human modification emerged in remote sectors during the Second Wave, some dangerously aggressive in their proselytization. **Eugenics cultists** during the Mandate's decline sought wholesale improvement of the human genetic legacy despite terrible costs in maladaptive mutation.

## Factions and Flavors

- **Stellar domains / stellar unions** — loose confederacies forming around the strongest worlds, or small empires of colonies held under one dominant neighbor. Border skirmishes between rival space empires are becoming more common.
- **Scavenger Fleets** — descended from the armadas of spike drive ships that banded together around 2700 to survive the core-world die-off. Today's primary movers of salvage and interstellar commerce.
- **Psychic academies** — rare, jealously guarded, sometimes government-sponsored and sometimes freelance; the only path to trained psionics in the post-Scream era.
- **Alien powers** — in some frontier regions, the dominant faction is not human at all.
